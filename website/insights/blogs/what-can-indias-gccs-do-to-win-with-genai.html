    The GenAI Imperative: Why GCCs Must Act Now

    India is home to over 1,500 GCCs, many of which are already the digital and analytics engines of their parent organizations. But GenAI introduces a fundamental shift. It’s not just about automating tasks. It’s about reimagining how knowledge work is done,faster, deeper, and more human-like.
    What makes GCCs the perfect launchpad?

    • Talent Density: India’s engineering and data science talent pool is already among the most competitive globally.
    • Proximity to Enterprise DNA: GCCs are embedded in enterprise workflows, making them ideal to identify high-impact use cases.
    • Agility at Scale: With the right frameworks, GCCs can build, test, and scale GenAI pilots with unmatched speed.

    But acting on this opportunity is where most GCCs get stuck.

    What’s Holding Back GenAI in GCCs?

    Let’s be honest, GenAI has incredible potential, but getting past those flashy proof-of-concept demos? That’s where many teams hit a wall.

    1. Lack of Alignment with Enterprise Goals: GCCs often struggle to align their innovation roadmap with broader enterprise objectives, leading to projects that lack sponsorship or longevity.
    2. Unscalable POCs: Teams can prototype well, but scaling those prototypes into robust production systems remains elusive due to fragmented tooling, talent gaps, or governance issues.
    3. Skill Shortages: Even tech-savvy teams find themselves under-equipped to handle new GenAI roles like LLMOps Engineers or Prompt Engineers.
    4. Inability to Attract Talent: The talent war is real. Attracting and retaining niche AI talent while competing with startups and global tech giants is a tall order.

    These roadblocks are real, but they are also solvable, with the right partner and the right approach.
