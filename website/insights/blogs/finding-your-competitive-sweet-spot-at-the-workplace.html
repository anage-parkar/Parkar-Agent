Finding Your Competitive Sweet Spot at the Workplace!

    Do you ever look at some competitive colleagues and wonder how the heck they just ‘know’ which opportunities to jump at while you ponder away till the same opportunity is no more? Or are you the competitive sort who looks at your seemingly relaxed colleagues and yearn for the easy camaraderie they have internally while you “struggle” for the same despite being the top honcho in the workplace? Or maybe you have that hyper-aggressive colleague who comes across as two-faced, who stomps over all others and you can’t help but break your head over how the higher-ups can’t see or feel the toxicity while everyone else on the floor can?

    Workplace competitiveness is like a finely tuned instrument, where hitting the right note is essential. But it’s confusing to get the hang of how much competitiveness is enough.

    As always, here I am thinking out loud.

    1. Keep FOMO at bay:

    Not being competitive enough, although subjective, can feel like watching the office dance-off from the sidelines. To make sure you're part of the action and catch the eye of the decision-makers, you need to unleash your competitive spirit on the dance floor.

    2. Balancing the Act on the Workplace Tightrope:

    Excessive competitiveness is like trying to walk an office tightrope while juggling flaming torches - it might get you noticed, but it's not making you any friends. It's essential to balance ambition with collaboration to keep the show going. Also, it isn’t a bad idea to share the spotlight to maintain a supportive work environment while being competitive.

    3. Balance Ambition and Burnout:

    Striking the right balance is like enjoying a cup of coffee at the perfect temperature - not too cold (representing a lack of competitiveness that can lead to office hibernation) and definitely not scalding (signifying burnout). Your competitive spirit should resemble that soothing, just-right sip of ambition. Finding this balance allows you to remain engaged, productive, and focused without succumbing to the pressures of burnout or feeling like you're not doing enough. It's about working at a sustainable pace.

    4. Juggling with a flair:

    Balancing competitiveness is like being a skilled juggler - you need the right rhythm, a sprinkle of humor, and the wisdom to know when to share the spotlight with a colleague. This approach fosters teamwork and a collaborative work environment, where your competitive edge doesn't overshadow the collective success of your team.

    5. Embrace Your Unique Office #Quirkiness:

    ‍Remember, it's absolutely okay to be the unique puzzle piece in the office jigsaw. Your quirks are what make you stand out and contribute to the diverse and vibrant workplace. Embracing your individuality and using it to enhance your competitiveness can set you apart as an office work of art in progress. Be yourself, bring your distinct perspective to the table, and let your unique qualities shine.

    In the world of workplace competitiveness, finding the 'just right' balance can be a challenge- a little daring, a bit amusing, and a whole lot of finesse. Embrace ambition, collaborate, maintain a sustainable pace, share the success, and be yourself. It's how you create your unique and successful workplace story. 🎪💼
